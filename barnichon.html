Overview:

The unemployment rate is undeniably one of the most important health indicator of an economy. To develop a prediction model for the State I am currently working with, I did some research to identify the best method out there. There are three common approaches to tackle this problem. The first is regression type approach that uses historical time-series of unemployment rate. The second approach is based on the Okun's law of output and unemployment rate. The third is labor force flow approach.

This last method was introduced by two economists from the Federal Reserve Bank: Barnichon and Nekarda (2012). According to the paper, this method shows an outstanding result in the short and medium term forecasts. Therefore, I have decided to replicate this paper by writing the codes from scratch on Python with a goal to develop similar type of model for the State. Click here to the project files.

Step 3: Understand the theoretical model

Labor Force Flow model is built on two elements: the law of motion and hazard rate forecast. The law of motion describes how unemployment rate converges to its steady-state, and this equation gives us one step ahead forecast for unemployment rate given the next period hazard rates. Hazard rate f_t is unemployment outflow rate, and s_t is inflow rate at a given time.

Step 8: Granger causality test

In the Vector Autoregression (VAR) model, each variable is modeled as a linear combination of past values of itself and the past values of other variables in the system. VAR(p) model is a seemingly unrelated regression (SUR). SUR is a set of equations that has contemporaneous cross-equation error correlation. At first look, the equations seem unrelated but the equations are related through the correlation in the errors. 1. Granger-causality test : First criteria to use VAR for forecasting is that the system of equation has to be SUR.
